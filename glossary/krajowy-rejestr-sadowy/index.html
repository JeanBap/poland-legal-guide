Krajowy Rejestr Sądowy (KRS)

Poland's national court register maintaining legal records for all businesses, including company registrations, changes, and dissolutions.

The Krajowy Rejestr Sądowy (KRS) — National Court Register — is Poland's centralized government registry of all legal entities, maintained by district courts across the country. Established in 1991, it serves as the authoritative record of all Polish businesses, from sole proprietorships to joint-stock companies. Every business entity registered in Poland must appear in the KRS, making it the backbone of Polish corporate transparency and legal certainty.

For foreign investors and international business partners, the KRS provides verified, up-to-date information on any Polish company's legal status, structure, directors, shareholders, and registered address. Access to KRS records is public and immediate through the online portal (www.erejestr.ms.gov.pl), where searches are free and queries are resolved in seconds. This accessibility makes due diligence on Polish counterparties straightforward and reliable.

Registration in the KRS is mandatory for all limited liability companies (spółki z ograniczoną odpowiedzialnością — sp. z o.o.), joint-stock companies (spółki akcyjne — S.A.), partnerships, cooperatives, and associations. Proprietors of sole proprietorships (działalność gospodarcza) must also register unless they operate below specified revenue thresholds. The registration process involves submitting articles of association, shareholder resolutions, and identification of directors and shareholders to the competent district court.

Changes to registered information — including director appointments, share transfers, address changes, or mergers — must be registered in the KRS within specific statutory timeframes (typically 14 days). Failure to update the KRS can result in administrative fines and, in serious cases, may expose company officers to personal liability for transactions undertaken while registration information was incomplete or incorrect.

Poland's KRS is recognized throughout the EU under the European Business Register Interconnection System (EBRIS), which allows searchability across member states. For companies with cross-border operations, the KRS registration provides legal certainty and facilitates banking, credit, and contract negotiations across Europe.

Key Facts

  • Mandatory registry for all Polish legal entities
  • Maintained by district courts; public access via www.erejestr.ms.gov.pl
  • Registration required within 14 days of company formation
  • Changes to directors, shares, or structure must be registered within 14 days
  • KRS searchable throughout EU via EBRIS

Common Mistake

Many foreign investors assume their Polish company is fully operational once bank accounts and tax numbers are obtained. KRS registration and keeping records updated are equally essential — contracts signed by unregistered directors or entities may not be enforceable.

Extra Tip

Request a KRS extract (wypis z KRS) when signing significant contracts with Polish counterparties. The extract is current, dated, and carries evidential weight confirming the company's legal status and authorized signatories.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is KRS registration free?

Registration fees vary by entity type (typically 100–500 PLN). Online searches and extracts are free for all users.

How long does KRS registration take?

Registration typically completes within 7–14 working days after submission by your lawyer or the company.

What happens if we don't update KRS records?

Outdated records may invalidate contracts, expose directors to personal liability, and result in fines from tax or court authorities.
