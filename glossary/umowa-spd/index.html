Umowa Spółki Sp. z o.o.

The founding agreement for a Polish limited liability company (spółka z ograniczoną odpowiedzialnością), the most popular business structure for foreign investors in Poland.

The Umowa Spółki (articles of association) is the foundational legal document for a Polish limited liability company (sp. z o.o.) — Poland's equivalent of a GmbH, SARL, or LLC. It establishes the company's name, purpose, registered office, share capital structure, shareholder rights, management structure, and operational rules. The sp. z o.o. is the most popular corporate form for foreign investors because it offers limited liability, flexible equity structures, and efficient tax treatment.

Polish law (Kodeks Spółek Handlowych) requires the articles of association to contain minimum mandatory provisions: the company name (ending in "sp. z o.o."), its registered office, business purpose, share capital (minimum 1 PLN in total, with no single share minimum), and identification of initial shareholders. Articles may also address profit distribution, transfer restrictions on shares, board composition, shareholder dispute resolution, and voluntary liquidation procedures.

Articles of association must be executed before a notary public (notariusz) in Poland and registered with the National Court Register (KRS) within 14 days. For foreign investors, a Polish lawyer typically drafts the articles, coordinates notarization, and manages KRS registration. The articles may be drafted in Polish or English; notarization requirements and KRS registration procedures are the same either way.

Amendments to articles require extraordinary shareholder resolutions (passed by shareholders holding at least two-thirds of voting rights unless the articles specify a higher threshold) and must be notarized and registered in the KRS within 14 days. Common amendments include capital increases, profit distribution changes, and expansion of business purposes.

Liability in an sp. z o.o. is limited to shareholders' contributed capital. Personal assets of shareholders and directors are not at risk for company debts or liabilities — a key distinction from sole proprietorships. This limited liability protection is lost only if shareholders personally guarantee company obligations or if piercing the corporate veil is justified by fraud or illegality.

Key Facts

  • Required for all Polish sp. z o.o. (limited liability companies)
  • Minimum share capital: 1 PLN total (no single share minimum)
  • Must be notarized and registered in KRS within 14 days
  • Shareholders' liability limited to capital contributed
  • Can be drafted in Polish or English

Common Mistake

Underestimating the importance of clear articles addressing share transfer rights and profit distribution. Vague or missing provisions lead to disputes when new investors join or profits are declared. Draft comprehensive articles with your lawyer from the outset.

Extra Tip

If you anticipate future capital increases or investor changes, include favorable amendment provisions in your initial articles. Lower thresholds for shareholder approval of certain amendments can expedite restructuring without full renegotiation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can we change the articles after formation?

Yes, by extraordinary shareholder resolution (typically 2/3 majority). The amendment must be notarized and registered in the KRS.

What if we don't have articles in Polish?

Articles can be drafted and notarized in English or other languages, but you must register an official Polish translation with the KRS.

Can articles restrict share transfers?

Yes. Articles commonly require board or shareholder approval for transfers, preemption rights for existing shareholders, or restrictions on foreign ownership. Such clauses are enforceable if clearly stated.
